CHAPTER TWO
The Truth That Found Its Way Through

The following morning Jason met his old friend Lorenzo for coffee. He arrived with the familiar habit of masking exhaustion beneath small talk. It was a skill he had perfected over the years. He knew how to appear steady even when his spirit felt anything but steady.

Yet the moment he sat down, Lorenzo studied him with a quiet familiarity that softened every wall Jason tried to keep in place.

What followed was not a dramatic outpouring. It was simple honesty.

Jason admitted that his spirit felt heavy. He described a weight he could not fully explain. He spoke about the ache beneath his silence.

He talked slowly at first, but as the words came, so did the tears.

He wiped them away without apology.

Speaking his heart to someone who had known him for years brought a small sense of relief, even though the questions inside him still waited for answers.

Lorenzo listened without interrupting.

When Jason finished, his friend reminded him that even Jesus stepped away from the crowds when the demands of people pressed too hard.

Exhaustion is not failure.

It is often the moment that invites a believer into deeper honesty, where God can be heard in the quiet instead of the noise.

When Jason returned home, something gentle nudged him toward the drawer he rarely touched.

He opened it and pulled out the old hymnal he had kept from his grandmother’s house. The book felt warm in his hands, as if the memories within it had been waiting for him.

He opened to a familiar page. The lyrics rose from his lips in a trembling whisper.

Each word stirred something he thought had gone silent, yet it was still alive beneath the years of distance.

An editor reading his story would mark this moment with care.

Not because of anything dramatic he said, but because of what he finally allowed himself to feel.

The hymnal became the first thread of his awakening.

It became the gentle pull God used to draw him back to the center of himself.
